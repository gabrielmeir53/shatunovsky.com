The New Standard: Enforcing Economic Meritocracy, Modernizing Digital Rights, and Elevating Workplace Conditions Within the NFL

Sports Law (Prof. Steve Ross) · Penn State Dickinson Law · Fall 2025

This paper proposes a shift in NFL collective bargaining strategy from protecting a fixed share of league revenue to pursuing structural reforms through enforceable CBA amendments. It identifies three areas where the 2020 CBA leaves systemic problems unaddressed—player digital rights, cross-club facility disparities, and revenue-sharing free-rider dynamics—and proposes concrete mechanisms including codified facility standards with an escrow-backed enforcement remedy. The legal analysis draws on Ford Motor Co. v. NLRB, American Needle v. NFL, and the NLRA’s mandatory-bargaining framework.

The Loper Rorschach Test: Maximalism, Bifurcation, and the Circuit Split Over Agency Deference

Standards of Review Seminar (Prof. Jamison Colburn) · Penn State Dickinson Law · Fall 2025

This paper analyzes the competing models of judicial review that federal circuits have adopted following the Supreme Court’s decision in Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo. It contrasts the Sixth Circuit’s “Maximalist” approach in Ohio Telecom v. FCC, which collapses technical agency judgments into pure questions of law, with the D.C. and Second Circuits’ “Bifurcated” model in Sprint and Verizon, which preserves deference to agency fact-finding within judicially defined statutory boundaries. The paper argues that Seven County Infrastructure Coalition v. Eagle County validates the Bifurcated model.

Unpublished manuscript; excerpted for writing sample.
